BSDA Disclaimer

As part of SEBI's initiative to encourage retail participation and simplify stock market investing, SEBI has introduced the Basic Services Demat Account (BSDA). According to the circular click here, starting from 1st September 2024, demat accounts will automatically be considered under the BSDA category upon opening.

After your account is opened, your eligibility for BSDA will be determined by the depository (NSDL/CDSL). Based on their review, your account will either be categorized as BSDA, click here to check BSDA tariff sheet or remain a normal demat account click here to check Arihant plus standard tariff sheet.

  • If your account is categorized as BSDA, the AMC (Annual Maintenance Charges) will be applied as per BSDA guidelines:
    • NIL AMC if the value of holdings is below ₹4,00,000.
    • ₹100 AMC if the value of holdings is between ₹4,00,001 and ₹10,00,000.
    • If the value of holdings exceeds ₹10,00,001, the scheme you opted while opening an account will be applicable.
  • If your account is categorized outside of BSDA (Normal category), AMC will be charged as per Arihant plus standard tariff sheet.

Trade responsibly in Futures & Options

  • 9 out of 10 individual traders in Futures and Options Segment, incurred net losses.
  • On an average, loss makers registered net trading loss close to ₹ 50,000.

Source: SEBI study dated January 25, 2023 on “Analysis of Profit and Loss of Individual Traders dealing in Futures and Options (F&O) Segment”, wherein Aggregate Level findings are based on annual Profit/Loss incurred by individual traders in F&O during FY 2021-22.
